Historical Places In India

India is a country with a vast history and many historical monuments to visit.

HAWA MAHAL

Hawa Mahal, Rajasthan

The Hawa Mahal is a place in the city of Jaipur, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana,or women's chamber. It is regarded as the signature building of Rajasthan and was built by Maharaj Sawai Pratap Singh.

Best Time To Visit: October To March
Nearest Railway Station: Jaipur Railway Station ( 6 km )
Nearest Airport: Jaipur Airport ( 11 km )

GOLDEN TEMPLE

Golden Temple, Amritsar

he Golden Temple( also known as the Harmandir Saheb ) , is a gurudwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India It is the preeminent spiritual site of Sikhism. It is one of the holiest sites in Sikkism, alongside the Gurudwara Darbar Sahib Kartarpur in Kartarpur and Gurudwara Janam Asthan in Nankana Sahib

Best Time To Visit: November To March
Nearest Railway Station: Puri Railway Station ( 30 km )
Nearest Airport: Shri Guru Ram Das Jee International Airport of Amritsar ( 11 km )

INDIA GATE

India Gate, Delhi

The All India War Memorial, popularly known as the India Gate, is a war memorial located in New Delhi. It is dedicated to the 82,000 soldiers, both Indian and British, who died duting the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The Amar Jawan Jyoti is the burning structure, right underneath the archway, which symbolizes the eternal, immortal soldiers of India.

Best Time To Visit: February To April and August To November
Nearest Railway Station: Amritsar Railway Station ( 2 km )
Nearest Airport: IGI Airport ( 13 km )

MYSORE PALACE

Mysore Palace, Mysore

The former residence of the royal family of Mysore. The Mysore Palace is an extremely breath-taking example of the Indo-Saracenic style of architecture. It was built in the year 1912 for the 24th Ruler of the Wodeyar Dynasty and is till date on of the biggest palaces in the country. It's one of the most visited and well-known tourist attractions in India.

Best Time To Visit: March To July
Nearest Railway Station: Mysore Junction ( 2.5 km )
Nearest Airport: Mysuru Airport ( 10 km )

JANTAR MANTAR

Jantar Mantar, Delhi

Jantar Mantar in Delhi is the largest stone astronomical observatory in the World. It is located in modern city of Delhi. Built during the period between 1727 nd 1733, the Jantar Mantar is still in running condition and is stands as a witness regarding the wisdom of former age.

Best Time To Visit: October To March
Nearest Railway Station: New Delhi Railway Station
Nearest Metro Station: Patel Chowk
Nearest Airport: Indira Gandhi International Airport

SUN TEMPLE

Sun Temple, Konark

Dedicated to Lord Surya, this 13th century temple is an architectural marvel and the quintessential example of Oriyan form of architecture. It is believed that the temple was constructed by King Narasimhadeva I of Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The shape of the temple is of a massive chariot with sumptuously engraved stone wheels, pillars and walls. A major part of the structure at present is in ruins. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Best Time To Visit: September To March
Nearest Railway Station: Puri Railway Station ( 30 km )
Nearest Airport: Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Airport, Ahemdabad ( 94.3 km )
